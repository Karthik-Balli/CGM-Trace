Metrics

Metric Definition
Mean The average of a set of numbers, calculated by summing all the values and dividing by the total count.
Standard Deviation (SD) A measure of the amount of variation or dispersion in a set of numbers, calculated as the square root of the variance.
Standard Deviation of Blood Glucose Rate of Change (SDR) The standard deviation of the rate of change of blood glucose values over a specific time period. It provides an indication of the variability in blood glucose fluctuations.
Interquartile Range (IQR) The range between the first quartile (Q1) and the third quartile (Q3) in a sorted set of numbers, representing the middle 50% of the data. It is a measure of the spread of the data that is less sensitive to outliers than the range.
Glycemic Variability (GV) A measure of the degree of fluctuation in blood glucose levels over a specific time period. It can be assessed using various metrics such as standard deviation, coefficient of variation, or mean absolute glucose change.
Time in Range (TAR), Time in Target Range (TIR), Time Below Range (TBR) These metrics are used to evaluate the percentage of time spent within different blood glucose ranges. TAR refers to the percentage of time spent within a specific target range, TIR indicates the percentage of time spent above the lower limit of the target range, and TBR represents the percentage of time spent below the target range.
Mean of Daily Differences (MODD) A metric that quantifies the average magnitude of glucose fluctuations within a day by calculating the absolute differences between consecutive glucose values and then taking the mean of these differences.
Continuous Glucose Monitoring Analysis (CONGA) A method to assess glucose variability by analyzing the differences between glucose values at different time intervals, such as CONGA-1 (1-hour interval), CONGA-2 (2-hour interval), etc.
Mean Absolute Glucose Excursions (MAGE) A metric that calculates the average of the largest upward and downward glucose excursions from the mean blood glucose value. It quantifies the magnitude of glucose fluctuations.
Hyperglycemic Events Episodes or periods of high blood glucose levels above a specified threshold. The definition of hyperglycemic events can vary depending on the specific threshold used.
Hypoglycemic Events Episodes or periods of low blood glucose levels below a specified threshold. The definition of hypoglycemic events can vary depending on the specific threshold used.
Interday Glucose Trends Patterns or trends observed in blood glucose levels over multiple days. This analysis can reveal insights into daily variations and long-term glucose control.
Overall Glucose Trend The general direction or pattern of blood glucose levels over a specific period, such as increasing, decreasing, or stable. It provides an overview of the overall glucose control.
